# Monkey Maghees — For-Sale Marketing Website

## Project Overview

Marketing website for the asset sale of **Monkey Maghees**, a high-revenue bar/pub located in Poughkeepsie, NY. This is an **asset sale only** — not real estate. All licenses (liquor, health, food) are in place and transferable. The goal is a polished, conversion-focused site that sells the business opportunity by leading with ROI and revenue data.

**Listing Agent:** Ryan Sylvestri — TheReelistRealtor (NY Licensed Associate Real Estate Broker)
**Brand:** Sylvestri Systems (website build + automation)
**Location:** Poughkeepsie, NY (Dutchess County, Hudson Valley)

## Business Profile

- **Type:** Cash bar / pub with small kitchen (limited food menu)
- **Sale Type:** Asset sale only (business, equipment, licenses, goodwill — no real estate)
- **Licenses:** Liquor license, health permit, food service permit — all active and in place
- **Revenue:** $300,000–$500,000/year gross
- **Daily Traffic:** 200–400+ customers/day
- **Cash Business:** Primarily cash transactions
- **History:** Established venue with significant local history. Previously listed/sold by Ryan.

## Website Goals

1. **Lead with ROI** — The #1 selling point is how much money this business produces relative to the investment. Revenue figures, daily traffic, and cash-flow potential should dominate above the fold.
2. **Showcase the opportunity** — Turnkey operation, licenses in place, loyal customer base, proven revenue.
3. **Visual storytelling** — Use uploaded images and historical context to build emotional connection and credibility.
4. **Generate buyer inquiries** — Clear CTA to contact Ryan / schedule a showing / request financials.
5. **Fair housing & compliance** — This is a commercial asset sale, not residential. No fair-housing language concerns, but avoid fabricating any financial data. All numbers must come from provided documents or be clearly labeled as estimates.

## Rules & Constraints

1. **Never fabricate financial data.** All revenue, expense, and profit numbers must come from documents Ryan provides. If no document supports a number, label it as "estimated" or "approximate" and flag it for Ryan's review.
2. **No specific asking price on the website** unless Ryan explicitly provides one and approves it for public display.
3. **No guarantees of future performance.** Use language like "historical revenue," "demonstrated earning potential," not "you will make X."
4. **Commercial sale language only.** This is a business/asset sale — no residential real estate disclosures apply.
5. **Image optimization required.** Compress all uploaded images for web. Serve WebP where possible.
6. **Mobile-first.** Design for phone screens first, scale up.
7. **Ryan's contact info only.** No third-party broker references unless instructed.
